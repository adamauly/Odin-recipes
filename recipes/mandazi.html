Oven-baked Zanzibari Mandazi Recipe

Description

Mandazi are mildly sweet East African doughnuts that are usually deep-fried. Usually they are leavened with yeast but they can also be made with chemical leaveners. The can be eaten as a sweet snack or with a savory sauce. If desired they can be rolled with sesame seeds before baking.

Ingredients

  • Flour
  • Salt
  • Sugar
  • Yeast
  • Oil
  • Coconut milk
  • Egg

An additional egg is required for the egg wash.

Method

  1. To make mandazi, combine all the dry ingredients together; including the flour, salt, sugar, and yeast. Combine the wet ingredients in a separate bowl; the oil, coconut milk and a beaten egg.
  2. Work the dough with a spatula until it comes together before placing it on a floured surface. Continue kneading the dough until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  3. Clean the bowl and coat with oil before placing the dough back into it. Leave to rise for 2-3 hours before shaping.
  4. Divide the dough into 8 pieces and leave to proof for another 1-2 hours.
  5. Brush the mandazi with an eggwash and place in a 220 oC oven
